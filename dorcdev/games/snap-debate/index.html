The Chaotic Party Debate Game.

In Snap Debate, you challenge your friends in fast-paced debates, sabotage each other with clever words and images, and see who reigns supreme!

What is Snap Debate?

Snap Debate is the chaotic party debate game where two players face off in ridiculous, high-stakes debates like...

"Should socks and sandals be illegal?"

But there’s a twist: While one player proves their point, the other gets to Snap them by giving them random words and goofy images they have to incorporate in their debate, to throw them off. Stay sharp and think fast, because you have a limited amount of time to prove your point, and players rate your performance on argument quality and snap incorporation before casting their final vote. Whether you're making a great case or barely surviving your Snap, only one player can come out as the ultimate debater.

How to Play?

Start a Game

  1. Someone (the host) downloads a PC (Windows, Mac, Linux) version of the game and hosts a game.
  2. 4-9 players on the PC or Mobile version join the game using the game code. If playing in person, the host's screen should be visible to all players. If you’re playing remote, the host can use a screen sharing app like Discord or Zoom.
  3. Once everything is set up, and all players are in, the first player to join, aka. the VIP, starts the game using their device.

Play the Game

  1. Each round, multiple debates will happen where players are paired up face to face in ridiculous debates.
  2. While one player in a debate is debating, the other player can sabatoge, aka. Snap, their debate. On their device, they can choose to Snap with an image or a word. After they Snap, the image or word will appear on the host's screen, and the player debating will have to relate their debate to the Snap.
  3. After each players' turn to debate, the other players will vote on how good their debate was and how well they incorporated the Snaps.
  4. After both of the players paired up in a debate have debated, the other players will vote on who's debate was better and/or correct.
  5. After the players vote on who's debate was better and/or correct, the scores are talled up and the winner of the debate is revealed.
  6. The process repeats until all players have debated, in which, their scores on the leaderboard are revealed and the round is over.
